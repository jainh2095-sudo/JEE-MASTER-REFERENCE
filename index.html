Miscellaneous Properties

M1
Ostwald dilution law: Оұ = вҲҡ(Ka/C). Diluting increases Оұ but decreases [HвҒә]. Diluting a weak acid raises its pH (opposite of what students expect).
M2
Acid strength order (HX): HI > HBr > HCl >> HF. Bond strength dominates. For oxyacids: more O atoms = more acidic. HвӮӮSOвӮ„ > HвӮӮSOвӮғ; HClOвӮ„ > HClOвӮғ > HClO.
M3
Solubility rules (must-know): All NOвӮғвҒ» soluble. Chlorides except AgCl, PbClвӮӮ, HgвӮӮClвӮӮ. Sulfates except BaSOвӮ„, PbSOвӮ„, SrSOвӮ„. Carbonates insoluble except NaвҒә, KвҒә, NHвӮ„вҒә salts.
M4
Very dilute strong acid pH: 10вҒ»вҒ· M HCl вҶ’ pH вү  7. Water contributes 10вҒ»вҒ· M HвҒә. Total [HвҒә] вүҲ 1.414Г—10вҒ»вҒ· вҶ’ pH вүҲ 6.85. Never below 6 for any acid in water.
M5
Buffer capacity max: At [HA] = [AвҒ»] (ratio = 1, pH = pKa). Buffer works within pKa Вұ 1. Outside this range, it's no longer an effective buffer.
M6
Indicators: Choose indicator with pKIn вүҲ equivalence point pH. Phenolphthalein (pKInвүҲ9): WA+SB titrations. Methyl orange (pKInвүҲ4): SA+WB titrations. Litmus: not ideal for precise work.
M7
Amphiprotic species pH: For HAвҒ» (from HвӮӮA): pH = ВҪ(pKaвӮҒ + pKaвӮӮ). This is independent of concentration вҖ” a unique property of amphoteric intermediate salts.
M8
Ksp and temperature: Most ionic compound dissolution is endothermic вҶ’ Ksp increases with T вҶ’ higher solubility at higher T. Exceptions exist (e.g. Ca(OH)вӮӮ retrograde solubility).
M9
Isoelectric point: pH at which amino acid/protein carries no net charge. pH = ВҪ(pKaвӮҒ + pKaвӮӮ). At this pH, minimum solubility and zero net migration in electric field.
M10
Hydrolysis degree increases with dilution: h = вҲҡ(Kw/(KaВ·C)). As CвҶ“, hвҶ‘. But [OHвҒ»] = hВ·C = вҲҡ(KwВ·C/Ka) decreases with dilution. Paradox вҖ” degree goes up but concentration goes down.
